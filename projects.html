Non-contact rail wheel defect diagnostics using air-coupled ultrasonic acoustic emission (MxV Rail)
Air-coupled UAE (20–80 kHz), experimental validation, defect characterization.

This project, conducted in collaboration with MxV Rail, focused on experimentally validating a non-contact diagnostic framework for detecting structurally significant rail wheel defects using air-coupled Ultrasonic Acoustic Emission (UAE). The approach enables defect detection without liquid couplants and at operationally relevant stand-off distances.

Primary contributions centered on the design and execution of controlled laboratory and field experiments, including instrumented impact testing, sensor placement optimization, and repeatable data acquisition to establish physics-based ground truth. These measurements supported identification of defect-specific acoustic signatures and downstream multi-class diagnostic modeling.

  • Controlled experimental validation of air-coupled UAE measurements.
  • Defect-specific acoustic signature characterization.
  • Experimental support for automated diagnostic modeling.
